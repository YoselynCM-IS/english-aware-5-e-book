How to present your bibliography

 

First you have to take into consideration the kind of sources you can use, like books, journals and magazines

 

from the public library, school library, yours and Internet sites. You can also interview experts on the topic

 

and those can be your parents, your teachers, siblings, people you know or even try someone on the

 

Internet, always act safely.

 

Journals are specialized magazines and you can find them also on the Internet. There are journals of

 

each scientific study like medicine for example. You can also look for the word  
archives 
of
and there will

 

appear specialized studies.

 

So, when you write the title of a book on your bibliography there is a special order you have to follow.

 

There are several institutions that dictate different orders, the APA (American Psychology Association),

 

the MLA (Modern Language Association), the CHMS (Chicago Manual of Style), etc. Although in Mexico,

 

the APA is preferred, and chances are that’s how you know how to do it in your Spanish classes, we are

 

learning English and to present works in English and for English speaking countries (in case you solve a

 

certification of English or try to enter an English speaking university), so we will follow the CHMS because

 

it is more widely accepted in English speaking countries.

 

In general, book titles are presented like this:

 

Voors, William.  
The Parent’s Book About Bullying: Changing the Course of Your Child’s Life. 

 

Minnesota: Hazelden Information & Educational Services, 2000.

 

Last name, name (period) Title of book in italics (period) City of publishing house (colon) Publishing

 

house (coma) year of publication (period)

 

If there is no author, but an editor, add the abbreviation ed. after the name.

 

If there are two authors, the first author remains with last name, name; the second author starts with

 

name and last name, like this:

 

Van Heel, A.C.S. and C.H.F Velzeel.  
What Is Light? 
New York: McGraw-Hill, 1968.

 

If there is also an editor or translator, add Edited (or Translated) by, like this:

 

Hesse, Herman.  
Siddharta. 
Translated by Hilda Rosner. Harvard: New Directions, 1951.

 

When using a magazine or journal you write the name of the article or chapter like this:

 

Chow, Ray, “Translation as cultural resistance”  
deSignis no. 12 
, (2008): 145-158.

 

The title of the article is between quotation marks (“”), and you write the number of the magazine /

 

journal, the year in parenthesis and finally the page numbers.

 

When using a website, you write the name of the article like this:

 

––“What Is the Future of Energy?,”  
Ecologist: The Journal for the Post-Industrial Age 
in  

 

https://theecologist.org/2019/jan/04/what-future-energy

 

Now, just order it alphabetically and you are done!
