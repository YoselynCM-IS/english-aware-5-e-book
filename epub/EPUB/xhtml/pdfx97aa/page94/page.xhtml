Writing Awareness

 

AUTOBIOGRAPHY

 

An autobiography is the narration of your life by yourself. It is very similar to a biography, but instead of

 

third person it is told in first person. Another difference of autobiography and biography is that you don’t

 

know how your life will end, so the end of your autobiography depends a lot on your age. If you are young

 

you can end with your expectations for the future if you are older than 60 and happy with your life you can

 

end with the happiest recollection.

 

To make your autobiography follow this outline:

 

INTRODUCTION – Your past.

 

Write about your birth date. Where

 

and when were you born. Who are

 

your parents? Did you have any

 

brother or sisters? Were you the

 

first, second or youngest child?

 

DEVELOPMENT — Write an

 

anecdote about your early

 

childhood, something that made

 

you very happy or sad. Your first

 

Christmas, a birthday party, etc.

 

Write about your school, a special

 

teacher, how you met your best

 

friend, etc.

 

Write about how you started liking

 

what you are good at: a football

 

match, music, dancing, etc.

 

CONCLUSION – What you want

 

for your future. Career, travelling,

 

marriage, etc.
