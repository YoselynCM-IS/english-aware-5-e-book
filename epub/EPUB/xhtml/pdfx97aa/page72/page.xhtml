Writing Awareness

 

Before you print

 

Format

 

Depending on your teacher’s specifications, a research paper is usually handed in Times New Roman 12

 

with an interline space of 1.5. On the superior bar of your computer you can see a menu where the names

 

of the fonts are, hit on the arrow there and a list of fonts will be displayed. Select Times New Roman, or

 

any other your teacher has asked you to use. On the right of the font there’s a space for the size of the font,

 

once again hit the arrow and a list of numbers will be displayed. Select 12 or any size your teacher asks you.

 

To select the line spacing options, find the space with lines and arrows going up and down, like this:

 

Click on the right arrow. The one that

 

is not blue. A list will be displayed.

 

Click on 1.5 and you are set.

 

Page numbers

 

You can type the page numbers manually or through the function of Insert in your computer. Usually it is

 

preferred to type them on the bottom right corner, but you should type them where teacher asks you to.

 

Manually, just type the number at the end of the page on the right bottom corner.

 

Through Insert, click on that tab on the top of your computer. You will see several

 

options. Look for the one that says Page Number. Click on the arrow. A list will be

 

displayed with several options:

 

Click on Page Number. A window will open. On Position click on the arrows and

 

select Bottom of page (Footer) on Alignment,

 

select Right. Click on the square next to the

 

message  
Show number on the first page 
if you

 

want it to appear, many teachers or publications prefer not to have

 

a number on the first page. It depends on them. Your numbers will

 

appear on the right bottom corner on the page.

 

How to capitalize a title

 

Once you have decided on your title, you should write it properly. The general rules are as follow:

 

1.
Capitalize nouns, pronouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs, and some conjunctions.

 

2.
Lower case the articles  
a, an  
and  
the.

 

3.
Lowercase prepositions, regardless of length, except when they are used adverbially or adjectivally

 

(
up
in
Look Up, down in Turn Down, on  
in
The On Button, to  
in
Come To, 
etc.).

 

4.
Lowercase the conjunctions:  
and, but, for, or  
and  
nor.

 

5.
 
Lowercase  
to
when it is part of an infinitive, for example,  
to Go 
.

 

6.
Lowercase the part of a proper name such as  
de, von 
, etc. Like  
Víctor de la Peña.

 

7.
Lowercase the second part of a species name. for example, 
Acipenser fluvescens
