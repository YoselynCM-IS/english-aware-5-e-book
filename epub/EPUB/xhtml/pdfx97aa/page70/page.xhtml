Writing Awareness

 

Checklist for the first draft of your research paper

 

Instructions to use a checklist:

 

1.
Read all the tasks. That is what you should have done in your research paper. You can also read it

 

before you start writing so you can use it as a guideline.

 

2.
Check ( 
✓
) what you did. Cross ( 
✗
) what you didn’t.

 

3.
Do what you didn’t.

 

TASK 
✓ ✗

 

1.
Start with an introductory paragraph.

 

2.
Write your objective, main idea, what you are going to write about and why.

 

3.
Call the attention of your reader by starting with a question, quotation, particular information or  

 

 
any other interesting idea.

 

4.
Continue with at least two or three paragraphs for the development.

 

5.
Use plenty of data or information like names, sites, magazines, journals, books, numbers  

 

 
(quantities, distances, dates, hours, etc.).

 

6.
Use clear examples to explain complicated ideas or hard to understand information.

 

7.
Use drawings or diagrams when they were necessary to make a concept clear.

 

8.
End with a concluding paragraph.

 

9.
Summarize in the concluding paragraph your objective, the most important main ideas and one  

 

 
really necessary example (different from the one in the development)

 

10.
Use the dictionary to check spelling.

 

11.
Use a grammar manual, grammar reference in your book and / or grammar site to check your  

 

grammar

 

12.
The paragraphs are in order, maybe a paragraph in the development would be better before or after.

 

13.
Let the research paper rest before reading it again to see if it makes sense

 

14.
Let someone else (friend, classmate, older sibling) read it and listen to feedback. Act accordingly.

 

Change only if you think it necessary.

 

15.
Check all sentences end with periods.

 

16.
Check use of commas (in series, in numbers).

 

17.
Check use of parentheses (in extra information, in some explanations).

 

18.
Check colons when introducing series, when introducing a definition.

 

19.
Check use of quotation marks when you quote text from a source or something someone said.

 

20.
Check use of capital letters in titles of books, names and institutes.

 

21.
Check use of italics in titles of books, magazines, journals.

 

22.
Check use of apostrophe for possessive case and contractions.

 

23.
Check correct use of plurals.

 

24.
Check subject + verb agreement.

 

25.
Check correct use of auxiliaries and verbs. (does + verb without –s; did + verb in simple form;

 

modals + verb in simple form; have / has + past perfect, etc.)

 

26.
Check correct use of references, that is pronouns to refer to nouns. (she – women and girls; he –

 

men and boys; it – singular things in general; they – women, men, women and men, plural things.; etc.).

 

27.
Check use and order of adjectives.

 

28.
Check use and placement of adverbs.
